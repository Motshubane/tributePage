MOSES SHIMO SELETISHA

Introduction

Moses Shimo Seletisha is a radical performance poet and author. He is a native writer, an intellectual whose area interest is African languages and their context, Seletisha has chosen to restrict himself writing in no language either than Sepedi. By any means neccesary, Seletisha prides himself as having been initiated by some of Sepedi prolific writers such as Goodenough Mashego, Tebogo Maahlamela, Matete Motsoaledi and other. Some of his work has been featured in virious forms of literary journals, that includes Botsotso, megaArtist magazine, KaasikultureBlog, KaganongBlog, Poetrypotion, Spwetan, DailySun and others.

Small Biography

Moses Shimo Seletisha was born at Mooihoek Village in Tsimanyane. He grew up in Leeufontein next to Marble Hall in Limpopo Province. By his confession his father passed on when he was five-years-old, leaving his mother to raise him, a brother and sister with her R500 a month wages. He remembers that his father worked in the mines and alleges his pension was squandered by his uncle. As a result he was raised by his grandparents Martha and Stephen Seletisha.

He is one of a growing number of young artists who choose to express themselves in their mother tongue because as he says, “why do you want me to speak your language when I have mine? ”.
